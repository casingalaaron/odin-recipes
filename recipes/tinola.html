Tinola

Description

is a light and nourishing Filipino chicken soup characterized by its warming ginger broth. It is a staple comfort food, especially favored on rainy days or for those feeling under the weather.

Ingredients

  • Chicken: 1kg bone-in chicken peices (thighs and legs provide the best flavor).
  • Aromatics: 2-3 hums of ginger (sliced into strips), 1 medium onion, and 4 cloves of garlic.
  • Vegetables: 1 large green papaya or chayote (sayote), peeled and cut into wedges.
  • Greens: 1 cup chili leaves or malunggay (moringa) leaves.
  • Liquid: 5-6 cups water or "hugas bigas" (rice washing) for a richer broth.
  • Seasoning: 2-3 tablespoon fish sauce (patis), salt, and black pepper.

Steps | Instructions

  1. Saute Aromatics: Heat oil in a pot and saute the ginger, garlic, and onion until fragrant and traslucent.
  2. Sear Chicken: Add the chicken and cook for a few mintues until int turns light brown.
  3. Season: Pour in the fish sauce and let it simmer for 2 minutes to infuse the meat with flavor.
  4. Simmer: Pour in the water (or rice washing). Bring to a boil, then lower the heat and simmer for 30-40 minutes until the chicken is tender. Skim off any "scum" from the surface.
  5. Add Veggies: Add the green papaya or sayote wedges. Simmer for 5-7 mintues until the vegetagbles are fork-tender.
  6. Finish: Stir in the chili leaves or malunggay. Cover the pot and turn off the heat to let the residual heat wilt the leaves withoput overcooking them.
  7. Serve: Best enjoyed hot with a side of steamed white rice.
