Pork Sinigang

Description

Pork Sinigang is a classic Filipino sour soup known for its tangy, savory broth and hearty mix of vegetables.

Ingredients

  • Meat: 1kg (2.2 lbs) pork belly, spare ribs, or shoulder, cut into 2-inch chuncks.
  • Souring Agent: 1 pack (approx. 40g) sinigang mix (good for 2 liters of water) in 1 cup fresh tamarind juice.
  • Vegetables:
    • 2 pieces taro (gabi), peeled and quartered.
    • 1 bunch water spinach (kangkong)
    • 10 pieces string beans (sitaw), cut into 2-inch lengths.
    • 1 medium daikon radish (labanos), sliced into rounds.
    • 1 medium eggplant (talong), sliced.
    • 6-8 pieces okra.
  • Aromatics & Broth: 2 liters water, 2 large tomatoes (quartered), 1 large onion (quartered), 2-3 pieces long green chillies (siling haba).
  • Seasoning: 3 tablespoons fish sauce (patis), salt, and black pepper to taste.

Steps | Instructions

  1. Boil the Meat:Begin by boiling the pork in water. Skim off any impurities for a clearer broth
  2. Add Aromatics:Add the onion and tomatoes and simmer for 45–60 minutes until the pork is tender
  3. Add Taro:Incorporate the taro and simmer for about 10 minutes
  4. Flavor the Broth:Stir in the sinigang mix, fish sauce, and green chilies.
  5. Layer the Vegetables:Add the radish, string beans, eggplant, and okra, cooking for 5–8 minutes.
  6. Final Touch:Add the kangkong and cover for 1–2 minutes to wilt the leaves
  7. Serve:Serve hot, ideally with steamed white rice
